Methodology: Globex range = full overnight session high/low (17:30–09:00 Berlin). Frankfurt range = final 60-minute pre-open session only (08:00–09:00 Berlin). Both are compared to the RTH daily high and low to determine breakout type. "% of ABR" normalises the pre-market range against the 8-day rolling daily average bar range to make comparisons meaningful across volatility regimes. Classification is path-independent — it depends only on whether RTH exceeded each range high/low, so there are no unresolvable cases; all 1,878 days are confirmed.

The headline result — confirmed: RTH breaks at least one side of the Frankfurt range 99.9% of the time. Across 1,878 days spanning the COVID crash, the 2022 bear market and the 2024–25 bull, there is exactly one "Neither" day (2020-04-08, peak COVID volatility, Frankfurt hour abnormally wide at 39% of ABR). The market breaks both sides of the 1-hour pre-open range on roughly 5 of every 8 RTH sessions.

Both Sides Dominates the Frankfurt Range
When Frankfurt uses <20% of ABR (about half of all days), "Both" reaches 71.0%, climbing to 86.7% when the pre-open hour is under 10% of ABR. Even at the widest Frankfurt ranges (50%+ ABR), "Both" never drops below 38%. The Frankfurt range is simply too small to contain RTH on most days.
Bearish Tilt at Wider Frankfurt Ranges
When Frankfurt uses 35–50% of ABR, "Broke Low Only" runs at 24.3% — elevated vs the tighter ranges. A wide Frankfurt pre-open carries a mild downside directional signal for RTH, consistent with the v1.0 observation.

Profile: "Neither" days are overwhelmingly high-Globex-range days (avg 102.3% of ABR vs 61.2% population) where the overnight session has already covered most of the day's typical range. These are not quiet markets; the overnight session simply sets a wide enough envelope that RTH stays inside it.
Key Conclusions
Actionable takeaways from the v2.0 re-run on 1,878 days.
1. RTH Almost Always Extends Beyond Both Pre-Market Ranges
Globex broken 94.1% of days; Frankfurt broken 99.9%. Both rates are essentially identical to v1.0 (94.5% / 100%) on four times the data and a full multi-regime span — this is a structural property, not a small-sample artefact.
2. The 60% Frankfurt "Both" Stat Is Real
"Both" came in at 62.4% — higher than the original 59.7%. Conditionally it reaches 87% when the pre-open hour is quiet (<10% ABR). The "always breaks Frankfurt" rule has exactly one exception in 7.4 years, and it required record COVID-era overnight volatility.
3. Globex Range Size Is the Key Conditioning Variable
"Both" collapses from 42.4% (<30% ABR) to 3.4% (120%+); "Neither" rises from ~0% to 30.7% across the same span. The market commits to a direction — or stays contained — as the overnight range grows.
4. The Overnight Range Predicts Shape, Not Size
Correlation between GX range and daily range is r = 0.28. A big overnight range does not reliably produce a big RTH day — it predicts how RTH behaves relative to the pre-market range, not how far it travels.
