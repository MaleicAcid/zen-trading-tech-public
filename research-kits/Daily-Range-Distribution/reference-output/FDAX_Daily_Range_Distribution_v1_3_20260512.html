The distribution is right-skewed. The most common day (0.7-0.8x) sits below the average (1.00x), and a long right tail extends out to 3.24x. Half of all days fall between 0.74x and 1.19x of ABR — a 0.46x wide middle. Days at 2x ABR or above are rare (1.87%) but persistent: 91 separate days across 19 years.
What the stats words mean:
  • Median (typical day): the middle value. Line every day up smallest to biggest — the median is the one in the middle. Half of days are smaller, half are bigger.
  • Mean (average): add up every day's range and divide by the number of days. One huge day can pull this number up, which is why mean and median can differ.
  • Std Dev (spread): how much days bounce around the average. A small std dev = days are all similar; a big std dev = days vary a lot. About 2/3 of days fall within one std dev of the mean.
  • Mode (most common): the band that contains more days than any other. On FDAX that's 0.7-0.8x ABR.
